FAQ

See our Contest Information page for full details on the contest, format, prizes, and procedures.

When and where is the tournament held?

The tournament takes place at West Windsor-Plainsboro High School South, and this year (2017) the date of the event is Saturday, October 14th.

The "contest" part of the tournament takes about 30 minutes, depending on which grade you're in. Mini-events will last 1 to 1.5 hours, also depending on which grade you're in.

The entire event lasts a bit longer: students in grades 3 - 5 should arrive at 9:00 AM and will leave by 1:00 PM; students in grades 6 - 8 should arrive at 2:00 PM and will leave by 6:00 PM. We cannot accept significantly late contestants.

What should/shouldn't I bring?

Do bring a pencil and an eraser or a pen, your student ID (which you will receive by e-mail once you register for the tournament online), and your registration fee.

Don't bring a calculator, any rulers or protractors or compasses or drawing implements, or any common-sense things you wouldn't normally bring into a school.

Where can I find past problems to practice with?

We have problems and answer keys of all of our past contests available on our Past Problems page.

Where can I find event photos?

Our Photo Gallery has our 2017 event highlights. To see photos from past years, head to the Past Contests page, or check out our Facebook. Contact team@wwpmt.org if you would like to have your picture removed.

Who can I contact with any more questions?

If you have questions about the tournament itself (e.g. questions about the contest format and other general inquiries), please contact the WWPMT team at team@wwpmt.org and we will respond promptly to your concerns. For issues with the site, contact the directors of technology at webmasters@wwpmt.org.



There are only 100 spots open per grade, so register quickly!
